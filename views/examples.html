Real Phishing Examples

Learn from real cases of phishing attacks and how to spot them.

One of the most common phishing scams is the **fake bank alert**. Attackers send emails pretending to be from a bank, urging the recipient to verify their account due to "suspicious activity." The email includes a fake login page that captures the user's credentials. Always check the sender's email address and avoid clicking on links from unknown sources.

Another example is the **job offer scam**. Cybercriminals post fake job listings on popular websites, promising high salaries and benefits to lure job seekers. Once applicants provide personal information or pay a fee, the scammers disappear. Be cautious of job offers that sound too good to be true and research the company before sharing any details.
